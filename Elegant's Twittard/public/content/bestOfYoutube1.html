Best of Youtube | Part 1

Okay, so today I'm gonna talk about the best standalone videos on Youtube. Please keep in mind that it's impossible to write them all in a 'single sheet', so I choose to include the one which crosses my mind by the time I write this, and it's limited only to videos that I've seen. So if something is bothering u or u have better suggestions, just tell me in the comments section below. So here we go:

The Fable of the Dragon-Tyrant (by CGP GRAY)

Best short story video, if not the best video on youtube so far lol. At first, I thought it's gonna be about a dragon that kidnaped a princess and how the knight is able to save her or some sort. But no, the story goes with a very very unexpected turn, in a freaking good way. I'm not gonna spoil it nor interpreted anything, it's just too vast and complex to explain it in a short text. Go try to watch it, and I promise u, u gonna love every second of it. The narration and music are spots on, the atmospheric sounds blend very well with the intonation and pacing of the story. The illustration and animation are brilliant, proven by their choice of objects that accompany the narration, or the subtle animation that fits in with the illustration's tone. But what the best about this video, is no other than the story itself, the pacing is superb. Although it's an adaptation of another short story, the minor or major change here and there makes it almost like they are two different stories with a different message and moral lesson, but both are great in their own way, which version is the best? u decided on it. Yeah, some things are not adapted perfectly which makes it less logical, but all of em is just minor things, nothings change much.

The Death of Socrates: How To Read A Painting (by Nerdwriter1)

This is the best painting explanation video I've ever seen. It shows why this painting is great without making us feel like it's being too over-analyzed like any other painting explanation video does. Everything he said sounds perfectly make sense and he made it easier to digest without using too many complicated technical terms or vague examples that barely can be seen. And lastly, the painting itself is mind-blowing, literally, explode. Unlike The Mona Lisa which gets famous for many circumstances, mainly external reasons, this video only talks about the painting itself, not some special history or events that envelop the painting. Definitely check this video, if u still wondering why some paintings can get really famous, while all u can see is just a mediocre random picture.

Ahhh good ol 'RWBY, I really can't talk anything related to RWBY in a short way, but for the sake of the theme, I'm gonna try to make it as short as possible and eliminate anything that unrelated to this particular video in a separate writing. Oh, yeah, btw this is the best trailer ever, period. Oh, and the best fighting with the best choreography too. Upon the first time witnessing it, I feel really really amazed by everything they try to offer, and at the same time, I already declared that it's the best in some categories. Sadly, when I show this to anyone, they don't seem to like it, so I thought it just me that has a low standard, yea I know, perhaps they don't watch it to the fullest extend like, watching it in 144p, without sound, skipped it every 2 seconds, etc. or they simply watch it but didn't observe it, but nevertheless its a prove that this video cant be taken seriously cus perhaps its look so underwhelming, idk. So here's my opinion about it, why this Red Trailer is such an amazing trailer. I'm gonna start it with a breakdown of what I'm thinking the first time I watched it, and make a conclusion in the next paragraph. (But watch that trailer first so ur opinion isn't biased, and I suggest u watch it on 720p, use a headphone with 70% volume, and turn the CC on for maximum experience lol)

It starts with rather calming music with echoing voice sounds, then we see that the animation use red and white (and black (and blue?)) contrast color on the objects, 'the art style is interesting' I said, the petal and snow moves beautifully and made it looks like it's popping out if viewed in a good monitor, oh and if u can notice, its 30 fps and 3D animations are usually 12 fps, so it's already made a good score here. Then u can see a person wearing a red hood, which immediately makes me think of The Red Riding Hood, 'i might be wrong but it's gonna be cool if the character is based on that' i say. She walks away from the gravestone with her head facing the bottom which means she's sad or mourning. She walked down past a dark forest, which if I'm being honest, is not an amazing or detailed animation but just great enough considering this is a video from 8 years ago from a small company. The music beat sounds deeper right after the animation transition shows us several black wolves, it really suited well with the lyric too, 'Black the beast descends from shadows', and after the beasts jump at her, she's using seemingly some kind of magic and jump on top of them, then the music leaves the vocal alone and said 'gold..' while showing yellow ambient light of the moon, ahh perfection. After the short music drop, she shows her calm face, and the mechanical noise of the gun transforming fills the void, and after it's over, the upbeat music started again with a sign of the first headshot sound, ahh perfection. And don't forget that cool shaking camera works and slow-motion tho. Then, the action starts. He shoots 3 bullets, and if u have a good eye u can instantly understand that she's using the shoot's recoil as a way to propel or stopping herself. That petal from splatting blood tho, ugh. Here u can see that that shotgun thing can transform into a scythe. She hit the wolf but unfortunately, the blade didn't slice him, but after smiling, which shows that it's under her control, she pulled the trigger again and uses the recoil as a way to slice that wolf. And we got this majestic slow-mo wallpaper-able scene. She sticks her scythe on the ground and starts shooting rapidly, this preventing the massive recoil from that weapon although we can see that she's still taken back a little bit with each shoot. And when there's 1 wolf that succeeds in getting close, she unplugs the weapon from the ground and shoots that wolf which makes her propelled a few meters too, this, really emphasis the massive recoil that gun has. Then after managed to stand on top of her weapon, which shows us that the weapon is completely under her control, we started to see an S+ tier melee choreography which I won't talk about part by part. As always, she's using the recoil, and because the weapon is heavy, not just looks heavy, she uses her swinging momentum as a way to move her weapon or herself to attack or even dodge. This makes the fighting style a thousand times better than any character from another movie, anime, games, or anything that used an oversized weapon. Ugh, there u go, whining about unrelated stuff again, so, back from sidetracking. After she's getting punched, and the music started to slow down, we can see that the enemies number are still around 30, and now with a serious face, she reloads the weapon but with a different logo on the magazine, a black circle which is pointed by 4 arrows from different locations. I don't know what this means when the first time watching it, but after watching the series, that logo means its gravity 'dust', that gunpowder means it makes the recoil even harder. After she shoots it in the opposite direction of the enemies, the weapon launched her at such a high velocity to the enemies. The blur and the screen that getting whiter and gradually clearer EACH time she pulled the trigger which means a sudden increase in acceleration and deceleration is freaking cool, and don't forget the speed up music that exactly the same from the beginning too. If u look at it correctly, you'll notice that she change the form of her weapon to this, this allows her to spin if she pulled the trigger again, as u can see in the video. After that, she basically just gliding and spinning while occasionally controlling her weapon. And now it's ok to do a close-up face scene because: first, we already know clearly what she's doing, how and why she moves that way. Second, it shows that she's doing attack a lot faster than BEFORE, so if they animated it again, it's gonna be harder to differentiate which one is faster if all of the fights are always on a high tempo. Last but not least, this closeup scene only lasts not more than 2 seconds, while it still shows some zoom-out picture occasionally, which shows us what is she actually doing. Then the video ends with her posing, not too special but pretty cool with the raining of empty shells.

So, there u go, a mysterious badass character based on a famous fairy tale that has magic power and a freaking badass weapon that can change to 4 different forms and have some special bullets. Not to mention the kick-ass music that syncs with the best choreography and a unique fighting style. Yeah, the graphic itself isn't the best but the color pallet is cool enough to keep me interested. 'I can't help but wonder what kind of epicness the series gonna deliver' I think.

There's so much information being delivered in this short video but without spoiling any detail or huge plot, that's, what a good trailer is.
